# Research Sources — AI-Powered SEO Content Production

**Topic:** AI-powered SEO content production  
**Goal:** Collect high-signal practitioner voices (not generic listicles) to support a future B2B SaaS content playbook.  
**Selection criteria:** operators who ship, test, or publish original research; skeptical of AI hype; relevant to content workflows at a startup like 100Hires.

---

## Why this topic

The 100Hires role centers on **AI-assisted content with human judgment**. This topic maps directly: how teams use AI in SEO/content without publishing slop, how search is changing with AI Overviews and LLMs, and what actually works in production—not theory threads from people selling courses.

I prioritized **practitioners** over aggregators: ex-in-house growth leads, tool builders who run experiments, and consultants with named client outcomes.

## Cross-cutting themes (early synthesis)

1. **AI content works when edited** — Sam Oh, Ryan Law, and Kyle Roof all treat AI as draft/accelerator, not publish button.
2. **Freshness + citations** — Ryan Law’s citation research; Kevin Indig on AI Overview traffic impact; Lily Ray on updating evergreen content for real changes.
3. **YouTube as AI input and output** — Patrick Stox and Ryan Law: transcripts feed LLMs; YouTube is heavily cited in AI answers.
4. **Mid-funnel > volume TOFU** — Eli Schwartz: LLMs own awareness queries; SEO wins on comparison, evaluation, product-led pages.
5. **Workflow beats prompts** — Aleyda Solis and Michal Suski: structured prompts, templates, and QA steps—not one-shot ChatGPT articles.
